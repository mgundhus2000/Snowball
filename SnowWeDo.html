A field forgotten - How to restore old fields

There are many reasons that an older field might have been abandoned. Perhaps a farm property was sold and used for a nonfarming purposes such as hunting. Or perhaps the original family was no longer able or willing to farm the land, and the fields were left dormant for a decade or more. Whatever the reason, a period of five to 15 years is plenty of time for the tree, weeds and rocks to take hold of the land. That makes it unsuitable for farming.

The severity of the unwanted growth depends on how long the fields has been neglected and how aggressive the invading flora is. For this article, we will assume that the fields are overgrown and unsuitable for cereal equipment or production but still recognizable as a field – not a forest.
